Engaging

across generations

Books and their ideas are the seeds for reflection, conversation, and imagination. We will take our work of adoption and transformation as an opportunity to connect people with and around ideas, crossing barriers including age, race, and class. Concretely, we'll do this by:

  1. Hiring young people, especially at-risk youth, to help with the work of adopting and transforming books, simultaneously giving them valuable technical skills and judgement for their futures while engaging them with their rich legacy of words and ideas.
  2. Recruiting our senior citizens, who grew up in bibliocentric cultures, to engage with the new technology, working in concert with young people and connecting with a global community.
  3. Enlisting knowledge-worker mentors and advisors --- editors, writers, designers, librarians, attorneys, technologists --- to guide and inform the work of paid students and volunteers.
  4. Creating online conversations inside of e-books, both web-wide and associated with schools, libraries, and community centers.
