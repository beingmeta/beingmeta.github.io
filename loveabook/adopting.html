Adopting

books which matter

Some of the books we rescue will be public domain texts which have passed out of copyright. But others are "orphan works": works which are in copyright but out of print and generally unavailable. With electronic books, there is no good reason for a book to be out of print. We want these books to find the readers who will love them!

Often the electronic rights to these books are still held by or have reverted to the original authors. We want to find a way to engage those authors or their descendants in our project to make their books available for future generations. Sales of these books at modest prices will help support the ongoing operations of the project as well as providing a reasonable royalty for the authors (and much higher than they would be likely to get from a traditional publisher).

For either public domain texts or orphan works, adopting them for transformation will involve three stages:

  1. Picking "orphans to love," identifying in-demand out-of-print books. We will do this based on direct suggestions from our board and partners, through a web-based public voting system, and through a system for crowd-funding where individuals can pay to support the identification, adoption, and conversion of the books they love.
  2. Finding copyright holders, mostly by volunteer efforts of web researchers, librarians, attorneys, and para-legals.
  3. Encouraging and supporting these copyright holders in making their work available in electronic form.

These efforts will take advantage of a clause in the Copyright Act of 1976 by which copyrights sold after 1978 can revert to the authors 35 years later. This means that, starting in 2013, many authors will be able to make their work accessible to future generations on their own terms.
